Motivation

Oliver Stigley

7 April 2016

How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

This lens certainly made me think about who I am, and what I am doing in life. I'm not sure I came up with any substantial answers. I have many things that I like doing, that I'm passionate about, but I'm not sure the connection to purpose is as direct as I'd like. I enjoyed the segment on 'flow' a lot, as it put to words exactly they way I feel when I'm writing code, getting my hands dirty.

What are my core values

This question is significantly harder to answer than 'Search Inside Yourself' suggests. All I know, is that I'm constantly trying to make myself a better person, and create happiness in my life. I'm also not ready to have an open conversation about it either. I feel like I have nothing to say...!

This is not to say that I'm not happy, or that I think I'm not a good person. It's just that I think that it is important to consciously assess whether you could be handling situations more positively, and exercising and growing my emotional intelligence. I also think that happiness is something you create, not something you find. So it is important learn about yourself continuously, so that you know what happiness really means.

What do I stand for?

I find it hard to comprehend that we are lucky to be alive in the first place..... the chances are infinitesimally small. Then, while we are alive, we fly around a massive black hole at the centre of our galaxy, simultaneously floating around a gigantic hydrogen bomb riding on some neatly formed rock called Earth. I'm humbled by this, and I think we can all benefit from being a little more humble toward each other.

What else...

I love the concept of 'flow'. It resonated with my a lot, getting lost in problems and code, is exactly why I love doing it. I hope I can turn this satisfaction in to a wider positive experience and career.
