What learnings did you take from the chapter?

It's cool to know that we are reading a book that many Google employees also have to read as part of their emotional intelligence training. I particularly like the different ways of thinking about mindfulness, and attention. "paying attention in a particular way: on purpose, in the present moment, and non-judgementally"

I've believed for many years that emotional well being has a direct impact on physical well being. Not that I'm good at keeping things like stress and frustration in check.

I believe there is value in this exploring this subject. It offers insight, and it's healthy to explore with the goal of trying to become a better person. Something I always try, and occasionally fail, to do.

How did you find the two minutes of mindfulness?

Very good! I occasionally meditate, so I am used to the sense of calmness. I find yoga also helps focus the mind in to a state of calmness as well. For me, there is a link on focussing on the poses, combined with the difficulty and physicality of the poses, that clears the mind. I once described to a friend, that doing yoga made me feel like a puddle of calm water in a rough sea, with ripples of calm radiating out in to the rough. In other words, it makes me calm and happier, which then has an effect on others interact with.

I personally find focussing on my breath to be the best way to have introspective, calm moments. The 'Easier Way' of doing nothing but being for 2 minutes just wouldn't work for me. I have too many things to worry about! My breathing helps me not thing about those things, so I opt for that method.
